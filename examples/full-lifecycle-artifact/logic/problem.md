# Problem

## Field of invention
Self-closing door hardware; passive (power-free) door-closer mechanisms integrated into a hinge, with
controlled closing speed and an optional hold-open function.

## Problem (O01)
A surface-mounted overhead door closer is bulky, visible, and mounts separately from the hinge, which
complicates installation and detracts from appearance. A bare spring hinge returns the door but slams
it, because nothing limits the closing speed. Users also frequently want the door to stay open at a
chosen angle (for moving through with hands full) without a separate floor stop or hook.

## The gap prior art leaves (G01)
The cited art either (a) returns the door without controlling closing speed (a plain spring hinge,
PA01), or (b) controls closing speed with a separate, surface-mounted overhead closer that is not
integrated into the hinge and provides no in-hinge hold-open detent (PA02). No cited reference
**integrates, within a single hinge body, a closing spring, a hydraulic damper sharing the pivot
spindle, and a cam-and-follower hold-open detent**, with the closing speed set by an adjustable
metering valve.
