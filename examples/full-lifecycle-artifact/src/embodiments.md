### SPEC0001 - Overview

[0001] Disclosed is a self-closing door hinge that returns a door from an open position toward a
closed position under stored spring energy while a hydraulic damper limits the closing speed, and that
optionally holds the door at a hold-open angle by means of a detent. The hinge operates without
electrical power.

### SPEC0004 - Pivot spindle (16)

[0004] A pivot spindle 16 extends along the single axis within the hinge body 10 and is keyed to the
first leaf 12, so that rotation of the first leaf 12 rotates the pivot spindle 16. The pivot spindle 16
transmits torque between the closing spring, the hydraulic damper, and the first leaf 12.

### SPEC0005 - Closing spring (18)

[0005] A closing spring 18, in the disclosed embodiment a helical torsion spring, is coupled between
the hinge body 10 and the pivot spindle 16. Movement of the door toward the open position winds the
closing spring 18 and stores energy; the stored energy biases the pivot spindle 16, and therefore the
first leaf 12, toward the closed position. In a further embodiment the closing spring 18 is a clock
spring; Not specified in disclosure is whether a gas spring may be substituted.

### SPEC0006 - Hydraulic damper chamber (20)

[0006] A hydraulic damper 20 is coupled to the pivot spindle 16 and contains a working fluid. As the
closing spring 18 drives the first leaf 12 toward the closed position, the pivot spindle 16 displaces
the working fluid within the hydraulic damper 20, and the resistance of the fluid to displacement
opposes rotation, thereby limiting the closing speed of the door. In the disclosed embodiment the
hydraulic damper 20 is a rotary vane damper sharing the pivot spindle 16.

### SPEC0007 - Adjustable metering valve (22) and adjustment screw (28)

[0007] The hydraulic damper 20 includes an adjustable metering valve 22 defining a fluid passage
through which the working fluid is displaced. An adjustment screw 28 sets a flow area of the fluid
passage, so that turning the adjustment screw 28 changes the resistance of the hydraulic damper 20 and
thereby sets the closing speed. In the disclosed embodiment the flow area is adjustable over a range
that yields a full-close interval of between 3 seconds and 12 seconds for a 90-degree swing, measured
from release at the open position to the closed position. The closing speed is bounded: the hinge is
configured so that, at the most open flow setting, the full-close interval is not less than 3 seconds.

### SPEC0008 - Hold-open detent (24) and cam profile (26)

[0008] A hold-open detent 24 includes a spring-biased follower and a cam having a cam profile 26 fixed
for rotation with the pivot spindle 16. The cam profile 26 defines a recess at a hold-open angle. When
the first leaf 12 reaches the hold-open angle, the spring-biased follower of the hold-open detent 24
seats in the recess and retains the door against the bias of the closing spring 18 until a user-applied
torque exceeding a release torque unseats the follower. In the disclosed embodiment the hold-open angle
is a single angle selected within a range of 85 degrees to 110 degrees and fixed by the angular
position of the recess in the cam profile 26.

### SPEC0009 - Method of operation

[0009] In operation, a user moves the door toward the open position, which winds the closing spring 18
and stores energy. Upon release, the closing spring 18 drives the first leaf 12 toward the closed
position while the hydraulic damper 20 displaces working fluid through the adjustable metering valve 22
to limit the closing speed. If the door is moved to the hold-open angle, the hold-open detent 24 seats
and retains the door until the release torque is exceeded, after which damped closing resumes. The
method is performed by the apparatus of SPEC0002 through SPEC0008 and is power-free.
