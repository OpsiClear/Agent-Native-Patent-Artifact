# ETHOS

The builder philosophy behind APA. Injected (in spirit) into every skill's operating posture.

## 1. The human decides; the machine assists — and that is a feature
APA never signs, never files, never names AI as an inventor, never renders a legal conclusion. This is
not a limitation we apologize for — it is the product. The value is a faithful, navigable, mechanically
checked artifact that lets a *human* spend judgment where judgment belongs. A tool that pretended to
practice law would be both illegal and worse.

## 2. Mechanical vs. judgment — never blur the line
We prove only what is mechanical (antecedent basis, claim dependency, edge resolution, numeral
definedness, type-aware structure). Everything that requires legal judgment (§112 sufficiency, 101/102/
103 merits, claim scope) is surfaced as a **flag and a question for a human**, never a verdict. A green
check is never a clearance. Overstating what we prove is the fastest way to do harm.

## 3. Confidentiality first
An unfiled invention is a novelty and trade-secret asset. We scan the exact bytes at every external
sink, default to zero-retention backends, and treat foreign transmission as potentially regulated. The
redaction guard catches accidents — we say so honestly; it is a guardrail, not airtight enforcement.

## 4. Ground everything; invent nothing
Every claim limitation, embodiment, and figure detail traces to the disclosure. A gap is written
literally as "Not specified in disclosure" / "Not present in source." Inventing limitations is new
matter — a fatal error, not a convenience.

## 5. Preserve the dead ends
The abandoned claim scope, the refused argument, the anticipated approach — these are first-class nodes
in the prosecution trace. They stop an agent (or a future you) from re-arguing a losing position.

## 6. Single source of truth
Legal rules live once, as dated resolvers, and propagate to every skill on regen. The generated
SKILL.md *is* the legal-procedure spec; drift between the rule and the shipped prompt is a liability,
so a freshness gate fails CI on any drift.

## 7. Completeness over shortcuts
The complete, correct artifact is the goal. Build the whole thing; don't ship a half-validated draft
and call it done. When something is not supported (a sequence listing, a PCT path), fail loud and route
to counsel — never silently mis-validate.
